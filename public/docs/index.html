COMMANDER ORIENTATION // LIVE RULESET

Player Field Manual

Build an interstellar economy, turn uncertain sensor contacts into informed choices, and expand without feeding your fleet to the dark. Game of Worlds rewards deliberate risk—not blind clicking.

Turn-based multiplayer12 racesRisk-driven exploration
The central rule: information has levels. A nearby sensor contact is not the same as a fully explored sector, and an old report may no longer describe what is there now.
01

BOOT SEQUENCE

Your first turns

You begin on a homeworld with a Scout, a Colony Ship, one Metal Extractor, and an Orbital Turret. Establish income, identify a viable nearby world, and preserve the fleet that makes expansion possible.

  1. Read your home sector. Select the gold tile. The left panel is the source of truth for ownership, richness, terraform needs, buildings, and fleets.
  2. Choose an economic opening. Metal pays for most construction; crystal pays for probes, movement, specialist hulls, and strategic flexibility.
  3. Inspect the sensor ring. Adjacent tiles—including diagonals—show limited live contact. Probe promising or suspicious contacts before risking a fleet.
  4. Prepare local production. A sector needs its own Spaceport before it can build ships. Construction occurs at the selected owned sector.
  5. End the turn when ready. Turns also advance on the server timer. In multiplayer, End Turn records your readiness; it does not silently skip everyone else.

Quick matches

The standard turn clock is 180 seconds. Make a useful decision, verify the selected sector, then signal readiness.

Epic matches

Long turns suit asynchronous play. Standing orders matter more because you may be away when the server advances.

02

SENSOR DOCTRINE

Exploration & intelligence

Fog of war is graduated. Clicking a tile should tell you both what is known and how it became known.

Unknown

No reliable intelligence. Choose a probe or fleet movement deliberately.

Passive contact

Your one-tile sensor ring can reveal terrain class, controller, and total presence—not full composition or resources.

Explored memory

A probe or visit created a fuller report. Dim tiles are old memory; fleets and ownership may have changed.

Owned / live

Your sector and nearby live contacts give the most dependable operational picture.

Probes reduce fleet risk—not all risk

A probe costs 300 crystal. It can reveal a sector without committing ships, but hazards and counter-intelligence can destroy it. Successful reports become dated as turns pass.

Before confirming movement: check the intel badge, report age, terrain, owner, terraform requirement, and whether the action is Move or Probe.
03

LOCAL LOGISTICS

Worlds, buildings & economy

Resources and research are empire-wide. Buildings, defenses, Spaceports, Warp Gates, and newly built ships are sector-local. Select the destination world before opening Build.

Metal

The backbone of buildings and hulls. Rich worlds make natural industrial centers.

Crystal

Funds probes, movement, some advanced ships, and flexibility.

Research

Spent on empire technology. Academies improve its income locally.

Building rules

  • Extractors, Refineries, Academies, and Orbital Turrets stack and consume building slots.
  • A local Spaceport is required to build ships.
  • Spaceports and Warp Gates are unique per sector; duplicates are rejected before resources are spent.
  • Ship construction is immediate. There is no hidden production queue or local Spaceport tier.
04

NAVIGATION

Fleets, movement & expansion

Movement follows a direct plotted line from origin to destination. Every crossed sector matters: known obstacles appear in route preflight, while hazards in unmapped space remain unknown until encountered. Select the destination, choose Move Ships Here, select ships, review the summary, and confirm. Crystal cost scales with hulls and route length; research and racial traits can alter it.

Colonizing a world

Colonizable planets are sector types 6–9. You need a Colony Ship and enough terraform capability. Hazards resolve before control: a fleet that does not survive cannot establish a colony.

Expansion checklist: Is the intel current? Does the route cross known asteroids or a black hole? How much remains unmapped? Is the destination colonizable? Can survivors defend it?

Warp Gates

A paired network of owned Warp Gates bypasses normal-space route hazards between established hubs. Gates accelerate reinforcement, but a captured hub can reshape the front.

05

EMPIRE DOCTRINE

Research & race identity

Research applies across your empire. Economy technology raises output; sensors and movement improve reach; Military Shipyards unlock heavier hull knowledge. You still need a local Spaceport to build a known hull.

Choosing a race

Every race changes strengths, costs, restrictions, or special behavior. Read both the advantage and restriction before confirming; restrictions are strategic identity, not flavor text.

06

THREAT FILE

Combat & hazards

Black holes

Absolute danger. An entering fleet is annihilated; a probe is destroyed. Discovery does not mean safety.

Asteroid belts

Each entering ship risks destruction. Once you control the sector, it becomes safe for your transit.

Combat uses hull power, race effects, defenses, and researched advantages. Do not judge battles only by ship count. Preserve scouts for information, screen Colony Ships, and avoid committing everything to stale intelligence.

07

COMMAND PLAYBOOKS

Strategies that survive contact

LOW VARIANCE

Cautious explorer

Build crystal income, probe likely hazards, and expand behind reliable reports.

TEMPO

Expansionist

Claim a productive second world quickly, then add local extraction and a Spaceport.

COMPOUNDING

Economic fortress

Stack income and turrets, research efficiency, then convert the advantage into fleets.

DISRUPTION

Mobile raider

Maintain current intel, threaten weak production worlds, and withdraw before the response.

Common mistakes

  • Moving because a tile is visible without checking whether information is partial or old.
  • Building while the wrong sector is selected.
  • Researching advanced hulls without Spaceports or an economy able to produce them.
  • Expanding faster than fleets can defend.
  • Saving indefinitely while opponents convert resources into territory and tempo.
